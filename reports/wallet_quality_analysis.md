## Executive summary

**The owner's core question — "consistent persistent edge, or lucky run?" — has a
three-part answer:**

1. **Wallet edges are real *measurements*, not pure noise.** Splitting each
   wallet's own history across *markets* (same period), the edge on one half
   predicts the other half: ρ=+0.22, permutation p=0.0006, n=221 wallets. This
   survives the price-level control (per-share ρ=+0.20, p=0.0009). Differences
   between wallets within a period are genuinely measurable.
2. **Persistence *across time* is NOT established.** On the clean test (pre-June
   data only, June excluded because cohort selection on the June leaderboard
   collider-biases anything that includes it), the H1 edge predicts the H2 edge
   at only ρ=+0.08 (p=0.15) — below the ρ≈0.19 detectability floor at n=166.
   **We cannot tell** whether wallet skill persists month-to-month at this
   sample size. It is NOT proven absent; it is unproven.
3. **The composite "consistency score" actively fails validation** — it
   ANTI-predicts held-out edge (ρ≈−0.18, p≈0.99 in all three designs). Root
   cause: consistency-flavored components (win rate, tight CI, breadth) select
   favorite-price buyers whose per-dollar edge is structurally tiny, while
   fat-tailed longshot wallets score low but carry higher realized edge. Win
   rate correlates NEGATIVELY with edge (−0.21) across wallets. **Do not use
   the composite score for any selection decision.**

**Verdict census (strict taxonomy, §2):** 38 CONSISTENT + 7 CONSISTENT_YOUNG
(45/250, 18%) pass the strictest bar (both splits positive AND bootstrap CI>0
AND edge survives removing top-3 winners). 55 wallets (22%) show NO positive
copyable edge at all over their own 6-month record. The rest sit between —
mostly REPLICATES_WEAK (both halves positive, CI includes zero) and ONE_PERIOD.

**Data discoveries that outrank the analysis itself (§0):** the entire ingested
trade history is **taker-only** (median wallet does **84%** of its trading as
invisible maker fills; 139/250 wallets >80%). The primary metric was chosen to
be immune to this (it prices exactly the visible taker buys the bot copies),
but cash-flow PnL, copy-exit detection, and any "how much does this wallet
really make" claim are all affected — see §7 follow-ups.

**Owner's gate for new-wallet discovery (P5): NOT passed — discovery skipped.**
See §7 for the reasoning.

## 0. Read this first — what the numbers can and cannot say

- **Metric**: per-wallet *copyable entry edge* = stake-weighted (resolution payout − entry price) per dollar over the wallet's visible taker BUYs, aggregated to (wallet, market) positions. This is exactly what copying the wallet's buys and holding to resolution earns — it matches the live strategy and is immune to the taker-only feed hole (below).
- **The /trades feed is TAKER-ONLY** (discovered tonight; `takerOnly=true` is the API default and all ingested history used it). Maker fills are invisible. Cash-flow PnL is therefore unreliable for passive-exit wallets and is reported only as a flagged secondary column. This also means paper's copy-exit detection is partially blind to maker exits — standing caveat, affects the copy_exits backtests too.
- **Survivorship**: all 250 wallets were selected on this very window (June leaderboard). Edge LEVELS are inflated by construction; only cross-wallet separation, splits, and held-out tests are informative. Any persistence test whose outcome half includes June is collider-biased — the clean test excludes June from the outcome side.
- **Price-level confound**: PnL/$ is not comparable across wallets with different entry-price habits (a 0.90-buyer can at most make +0.11/$; a 0.10-buyer up to +9/$). Per-share edge is reported alongside; correlate section quantifies the confound.

## 7. P5 decision — new-wallet discovery: SKIPPED (gate not passed)

The owner's instruction: search for new candidate wallets **only if** the
quality score demonstrably distinguishes real skill from noise. It does not:

- The composite score anti-predicts held-out edge (§3) — using it for
  discovery would actively select the wrong wallets.
- The only validated signal is *same-period* reliability of raw copyable
  edge. Discovery inherently needs *forward* persistence ("good last month →
  good next month"), and that is exactly the part that is statistically
  unresolved (ρ=+0.08, p=0.15, under-powered at n=166).
- Leaderboard-based discovery would additionally re-introduce the selection
  collider this analysis just spent the night removing.

**What would earn trust:** the cleanest possible persistence test is already
running — the paper loop measures post-selection, post-World-Cup wallet
performance on data none of tonight's numbers touched. At ~30–60 days of
paper data, rerun `wallet_quality.py score` with the paper window as the
outcome half (pre-registered thresholds suggested in the proposal doc). If
raw-edge persistence then clears p<0.05 with a positive top-vs-bottom
quintile gap, a *raw-edge-based* (NOT composite) discovery pass is justified.

## 10. Addendum 2 (06:40 UTC): two robustness checks on the headline results

**Event-cluster leakage control.** The §3 reliability gate splits by market
hash, so correlated markets of one event (e.g. flawfence's Korean-election
day, one WC match's spread/O-U variants) can land in both halves and inflate
"reliability". Re-run with EVENT-cluster hashing (event_slug as the split
unit, 99,846 clusters over 505,598 positions): **ρ=+0.213, permutation
p=0.0005, n=219** — essentially unchanged from +0.224. Same-period
reliability of wallet edge is NOT an intra-event correlation artifact.

**Which score components are the poison.** Correlating each component
(computed on the train half, pre-June design) directly with held-out edge:

| component | ρ vs held-out edge | n |
|---|---|---|
| bootstrap CI lower bound | **−0.208** | 166 |
| win rate | **−0.166** | 166 |
| walk-forward positive fraction | −0.132 | 83 |
| edge without top-3 winners | −0.112 | 166 |
| worst-split edge | −0.019 | 166 |
| raw edge (reference) | +0.081 | 166 |

Every "consistency" component is individually anti-predictive — they all
proxy the favorite-price trading style, whose future per-dollar edge is
structurally low. This is the full explanation of the composite score's
failure, and the constraint on any future score design: **start from raw
copyable edge (possibly shrunken/price-controlled), never from
consistency aesthetics.**

threshold combination (min positions 30/40/60 × min span 45/60/90d) — wallets
only shift between the CONSISTENT and CONSISTENT_YOUNG labels as thresholds
change which split design they qualify for:

|---|---|---|---|

| n≥60, span≥90d | 32+13 = 45 | 55 | 45 |
